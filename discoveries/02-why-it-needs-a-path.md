# Why It Needs a Path — Discovery Notes

- **Problem:** Electricity flows wherever it can — through you, into the table, anywhere a path exists. How do you make it take your path and no other?
- **Key idea:** Conductors (metals, especially copper) carry current freely; insulators (rubber, glass, plastic) block it entirely. A wire is conductor inside, insulator outside. Wire cross-section determines how much current the path can carry before heat becomes dangerous.
